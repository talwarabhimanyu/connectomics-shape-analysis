3D Shape Analysis for Connectomics

During the Spring semester of 2019, I had the opportunity to work on a research project with Prof. Hanspeter Pfister's Visual Computing Group at Harvard, under the guidance of Donglai Wei and Zudi Lin. Our work falls under the area of 3D shape analysis for large scale connectomics, and involved analyzing 3D models of cellular organelles (such as neurons) inside the brain, with the aim of (1) understanding the complexity of their shapes, and (2) categorizing them according to their shapes. Towards that end, we developed a simple shape descriptor for these complex 3D shapes, which provides an intuitive idea of complex 3D shapes, and at the same time preserves adequate details to be used for shape sensitive tasks such as shape matching and retrieval.

Background
